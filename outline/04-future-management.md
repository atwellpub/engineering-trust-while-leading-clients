SLIDE 41
The Story of the Stone Sorter

A man cleared his stony field with great care
• First, he gathered the largest stones into one pile
• Then smaller stones into another pile
• He continued until he had ten organized piles of similar-sized stones

His neighbors laughed: "Look at the fool fussing with his stones!"
• They simply tossed all their stones into one ordinary heap

---

SLIDE 42
The Builder Arrives

A builder passed by and saw the ten organized piles
• He immediately bought all the stones for 40 silver groshen
• The stones were ready to use, already sorted for his needs

The neighbors rushed over:
"We have similar stones! We'll sell them for just a few groshen!"

---

SLIDE 41
The Builder's Response

"Your stones I would have to first sort out, which would take much work, time and effort. These here are already sorted, just as I need them now. I would rather overpay for these than accept yours free!"

The neighbors tried to sort their stones afterward...
But it was too late. The builder had enough.

---

SLIDE 42
The Lesson

"Always keep the best order in all things! When someone then comes with an offer, they are sure to always go for the best order."

A later effort is often in vain.
Preparation meets opportunity.

SLIDE 42
Your Role as the Waiter

The waiter's responsibilities:
• Present the menu professionally
• Recommend the specials
• Answer questions about the offerings
• Help the client make informed choices
• Never pressure, always guide

Your job is to inform and present options clearly.

SLIDE 41
Creating the Dining Experience

Your focus is on the experience you provide:
• Present the menu clearly (your PDFs)
• Make recommendations based on their needs
• Answer questions professionally
• Give them space to make informed decisions

Your job is NOT to pressure them into ordering.
Your job is to create an experience where choices are clear and comfortable.

The Opening PDF: The Menu

Like a restaurant menu, your Opening PDF:

• Sets expectations for the dining experience
• Shows professionalism and preparation

During the project, you're taking notes (sorting stones):
• What patterns do you see?

• What opportunities would benefit them?
